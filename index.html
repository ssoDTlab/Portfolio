Feature Space-Based Center Constraint Loss for Improving Quality and Diversity in GANs

Sang-Won Seo, Hyun-Cheol Park

The 6th Korea Artificial Intelligence Conference

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) often rely on various empirical and experimental tricks during training, which makes it challenging to objectively demonstrate the effectiveness of newly proposed methods. To address this issue, we conducted experiments using one of the most fundamental architectures, DCGAN, in order to logically validate the effectiveness of the proposed central constraint loss function. By embedding the training dataset into a feature space and imposing a constraint based on the distance between the distribution center and the mean, we ensured that the generated images did not deviate excessively. As a result, we confirmed improvements in both quality and diversity metrics without requiring any modifications to the network architecture. This demonstrates that our proposed approach can enhance performance even in a simple baseline model, thereby supporting the generality and scalability of the method.

Improving Quality and Diversity of GAN Image Generation through Cosine Similarity-Based Diversity Loss

Sang-Won Seo, Hyun-Cheol Park

The 57th Spring Conference of the Korea Institute of Information and Commu- nication Engineering (KIICE)

In this paper, we propose a cosine similarity–based diversity loss function to address the common issues of mode collapse and the imbalance between quality and diversity in GAN-based image generation. The proposed method calculates the similarity among generated images in the intermediate feature space of the discriminator, suppressing excessively similar outputs. Furthermore, it incorporates adaptive weighting that reflects the differences from the real data distribution, thereby enhancing both quality and diversity simultaneously. Experimental results on the CelebA and LSUN datasets demonstrate that our model outperforms DCGAN, WGAN-GP, and MSGAN in terms of FID and MS-SSIM metrics. These findings verify the effectiveness of the proposed approach in improving image quality while preserving diversity in GAN-based generation.

Deepfake Detection Web Application

Built a web application that enables users to upload images and videos, automatically selects the most suitable model among three, and detects deepfakes. For videos, users could specify a time interval to analyze, and the system provided access to prior detection results.
Contributed to frontend development and implemented a Stable Diffusion–based residual analysis module: real images are consistently reconstructed during denoising, while deepfakes exhibit distinctive residual artifacts, which were leveraged for CNN-based classification.

Roadside CCTV-Based Vehicle Classification and Noise Estimation System

Developed a system that processes roadside CCTV footage to detect vehicles using YOLOv8, classify their types with MobileNet, and estimate the traffic noise level of the observed area.
Designed the overall system architecture and proposed an energy-based noise estimation algorithm, which improved accuracy compared to simple linear summation and produced results closer to real-world noise measurements.

ROI-Based Change Detection and Automation System

Developed a modular application that performs real-time video capture and anomaly detection, integrating ROI selection, object detection (Haar Cascade, Mean Shift), and edge-based analysis into a PySide6 GUI. Designed and implemented multi-threaded video processing, a TensorFlow-based anomaly detection module with frame-by-frame labeling, and an automation feature that triggers keyboard events based on ROI conditions.
